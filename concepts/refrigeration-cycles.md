# Refrigeration Cycles

## Vapor Compression Cycle
Used in standard household and industrial refrigerators, leveraging the [[joule-thomson-effect]].
1. **Compressor**: Elevates the pressure of the refrigerant gas.
2. **Condenser**: Cools the hot, high-pressure gas until it condenses into a liquid, shedding heat to the environment.
3. **Expansion Valve (JT Valve)**: The high-pressure liquid expands rapidly into a low-pressure region, dropping significantly in temperature.
4. **Evaporator**: The cold refrigerant absorbs heat from the target area, evaporating back into a gas before returning to the compressor.

## Closed-Cycle Helium Cryostat (Stirling Cycle)
These systems do not consume helium (no refilling required) and can operate continuously to reach temperatures around 5 K.
The ideal Stirling cycle consists of:
1. Isothermal expansion (heat addition at high temperature).
2. Isochoric (constant volume) cooling.
3. Isothermal compression (heat removal at low temperature).
4. Isochoric heating.

**Physical Operation**:
A mechanical displacer moves back and forth in a cylinder connected to a high/low-pressure rotary valve.
- High-pressure helium flows through a regenerator into the expansion space.
- The displacer moves, expanding and cooling the gas.
- The low-pressure path opens, drawing the cold gas back through the regenerator (cooling the regenerator for the next cycle).
- The target sample is cooled via a cold plate attached to this expansion chamber.
