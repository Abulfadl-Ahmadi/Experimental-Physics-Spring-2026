# Vacuum Materials

The choice of materials in a [[vacuum]] system is critical, especially as pressures approach the Ultra-High Vacuum (UHV) range.

## Material Requirements
- **Low vapor pressure**: Materials must not naturally vaporize into the vacuum. Metals are stable, while most polymers and liquids are not.
- **Thermal compatibility**: Must handle thermal expansion and contraction without compromising seals.
- **Low outgassing**: Must not absorb gases that slowly release later.
- **Chemical stability**: Must not react with the environment or generate gas when exposed to high-energy particles.

## Recommended Materials
- **Stainless Steel**: The standard for UHV. Strong, low outgassing, smooth pinhole-free surfaces.
- **Aluminum**: Easy machining, moderate strength.
- **Copper**: Good corrosion resistance, excellent thermal and electrical conductivity, easy to machine. Often used for crush-seals (gaskets) in UHV.
- **Kovar (Nickel alloy)**: Great mechanical strength at high temperatures.

## Materials to Avoid
- **Plastics, Polymers, and Rubber**: These are the enemy of UHV. They act as sponges for water vapor and other gases, causing severe outgassing that makes reaching ultra-low pressures impossible. They are only acceptable in medium vacuum applications as washers or O-rings.
