# Superfluidity

A superfluid is a state of matter (most notably observed in Liquid Helium-4 and Helium-3) characterized by **zero viscosity** and **zero entropy**.

## The Lambda Point ($\lambda$)
In Helium-4, the transition to the superfluid phase (Helium II) occurs at the lambda point, approximately $2.17 K$. 
- At this temperature, the specific heat capacity ($C_s$) spikes sharply, creating a $\lambda$-shaped curve on a graph.
- Below this point, the fluid flows completely without friction.
- Superfluids have infinite thermal conductivity, making it impossible to establish a temperature gradient within the fluid.

## Strange Effects
- **Fountain Effect**: If a capillary tube is placed in a superfluid bath and slightly heated (e.g., by shining a light), the superfluid will rush up the tube and spray out like a fountain.
- **Film Creep**: Superfluid helium forms a film one-atom thick that can climb up and over the walls of its container, eventually emptying it.
