# Thermometry and Temperature Scales

An ideal thermometer must possess sufficient accuracy, temporal and environmental stability, wide working range, and easy calibration. The parameter measured should ideally have a linear relationship with temperature.

## Temperature Scales
Conversion formulas:
- $°F = 32.0 + (9/5)°C$
- $°R = (9/5)K$

## Basic Thermometers
- **Mercury-in-Glass**: Uses liquid expansion. Working range 0 to ~300°C.
- **Gas Thermometers**:
  - **Constant-Volume**: Based on the Ideal Gas Law ($T = T_{ref} * (p / p_{ref})$). Deflects a calibrated pressure needle.
  - **Fluid-Expansion**: Measures vapor pressure changes.
- **Bimetallic Strip Thermostat**: Uses differential thermal expansion of two bonded metals to cause bending. Radius of curvature depends on the difference in thermal coefficients ($\alpha_2 - \alpha_1$).

## Calibration
Thermometers must be periodically calibrated against trusted references or fixed temperature points (e.g., Ice point 0°C, Triple point of water 0.01°C, Boiling point of liquid nitrogen -195.798°C).
